ANSWER

The main branches of computer science, as described in the context, include:

1. **Computer Architecture** – The construction of computer components and computer-operated equipment.  
2. **Operating Systems, Networks, and Embedded Systems** – Investigation of the principles and design behind complex systems.  
3. **Human–Computer Interaction** – Study of interfaces through which humans and computers interact.  
4. **Software Engineering** – Focus on the design and principles behind developing software.  
5. **Theoretical Disciplines** – Includes algorithms, theory of computation, and information theory.  
6. **Applied Disciplines** – Involves the design and implementation of hardware and software.  
7. **Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning** – Synthesizing goal-orientated processes such as problem-solving, decision-making, environmental adaptation, planning, and learning.  
8. **Computer Vision** – Understanding and processing image and video data.  
9. **Natural Language Processing** – Understanding and processing textual and linguistic data.  
10. **Cryptography and Computer Security** – Studying secure communication and preventing security vulnerabilities.  
11. **Programming Language Theory** – Describing computational processes through different programming languages.  
12. **Database Theory** – Managing repositories of data.  
13. **Algorithms and Data Structures** – Central to computer science.  
14. **Computer Graphics and Computational Geometry** – Generation of images.  
15. **Theory of Computation** – Abstract models of computation and general classes of problems that can be solved using them.  

Additionally, the fundamental concern of computer science is determining what can and cannot be automated. The Turing Award is recognized as the highest distinction in the field.
