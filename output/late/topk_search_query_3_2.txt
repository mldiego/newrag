ANSWER

The main branches of computer science, as described in the provided context, include:

1. **Computer architecture** – which describes the construction of computer components and computer-operated equipment.
2. **Operating systems, networks, and embedded systems** – which investigate the principles and design behind complex systems.
3. **Human–computer interaction** – which investigates the interfaces through which humans and computers interact.
4. **Software engineering** – which focuses on the design and principles behind developing software.

These branches represent key areas of study and application within computer science.
